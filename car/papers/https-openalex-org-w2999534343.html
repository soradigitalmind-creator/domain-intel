Gasdermin E–mediated target cell pyroptosis by CAR T cells triggers cytokine release syndrome

Year: 2020

Primary subgenre: car-t cell therapy research

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Cytokine release syndrome (CRS) counteracts the effectiveness of chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T cell therapy in cancer patients, but the mechanism underlying CRS remains unclear. Here, we show that tumor cell pyroptosis triggers CRS during CAR T cell therapy. We find that CAR T cells rapidly activate caspase 3 in target cells through release of granzyme B. The latter cleaves gasdermin E (GSDME)
