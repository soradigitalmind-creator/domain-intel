Are Consumers Myopic? Evidence from New and Used Car Purchases

Year: 2013

Primary subgenre: econometrics

Secondary subgenres: energy, environment, and transportation policiesmicroeconomicsorder

Summary: We investigate whether car buyers are myopic about future fuel costs. We estimate the effect of gasoline prices on short-run equilibrium prices of cars of different fuel economies. We then compare the implied changes in willingness-to-pay to the associated changes in expected future gasoline costs for cars of different fuel economies in order to calculate implicit discount rates.
