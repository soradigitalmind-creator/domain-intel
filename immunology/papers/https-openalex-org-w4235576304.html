Abstract
The first great revolution in immunology is credited to Louis Pasteur. Among the many achievements stemming from that early period is the elimination of smallpox from the entire world in 1977, a century later. The second great revolution, beginning with immunochemistry in the 1940s, is ongoing. Perhaps our counterparts a century from now will be pleased with the outcome.
