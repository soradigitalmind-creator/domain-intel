The Valuation of Risks to Life: Evidence from the Market for Automobiles

Year: 1990

Primary subgenre:

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Using hedonic regression techniques, estimates of the willingness-to-pay for changes in the risks of dying can be inferred from actual behavior in market situations involving risk-dollar tradeoffs. Thaler and Rosen (1975) pioneered this approach, obtaining estimates of the value of a statistical life using labor market data, in this paper we use the hedonic technique to obtain the first estimates
