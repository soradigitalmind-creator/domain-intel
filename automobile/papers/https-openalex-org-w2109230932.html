Eddy current damper feasibility in automobile suspension: modeling, simulation and testing

Year: 2008

Primary subgenre: automotive engineering

Secondary subgenres: electrical engineeringmechanical engineering

Summary: This paper presents the modeling, simulation and testing of a novel eddy current damper (ECD) to be used in vehicle suspension systems. The conceived ECD utilizes permanent magnets (PMs), separated by iron poles that are screwed to an iron rod, and a conductive hollow cylinder to generate damping.
