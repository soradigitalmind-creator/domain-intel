Mortality studies of machining fluid exposure in the automobile industry III: A case‐control study of larynx cancer

Year: 1994

Primary subgenre: environmental health

Secondary subgenres: material sciencecontrol

Summary: A case-control study of larynx cancer was conducted within a cohort of automobile workers exposed to metal working fluids, commonly referred to as machining fluids (MF). Results are based on 108 cases of larynx cancer and 5:1 matched controls. Risks associated with specific types of MF, as well as specific components of the fluids were evaluated.
