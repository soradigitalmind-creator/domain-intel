Clinical Symptoms of Sleep Apnea Syndrome and Automobile Accidents

Year: 1990

Primary subgenre: automotive engineering

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Patients with clinical features of sleep apnea syndrome (SAS) and self-reported sleep spells at the wheel do poorly in simulated monotonous driving. To evaluate whether drivers with defined symptoms of SAS (heavy snoring, sleep disturbances and daytime sleepiness) compensate in real traffic by careful driving or not, the rate of car accidents over a 5-year period was investigated.
