Safety of Lap-Belt Restraint for Pregnant Victims of Automobile Collisions

Year: 1971

Primary subgenre: automotive engineering

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Of 208 pregnant victims of severe automobile accidents, 28 wore lap belts, and 180 were unrestrained. Lap-belt restraint was associated with a reduction in maternal death from 7.8 to 3.6 per cent, and with an increase in fetal loss from 14.4 to 16.7 per cent.
