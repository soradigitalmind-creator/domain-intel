AUTOMOBILE OWNERSHIP ANALYSIS USING ORDERED PROBIT MODELS

Year: 2002

Primary subgenre:

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: A mathematical model developed to predict automobile ownership for individual households residing in New York City is presented. This effort is distinguished from previous disaggregate household-level automobile ownership models primarily by the use of ordered probit models rather than the commonly used multinomial logit (MNL) and sequential logit (SL) models.
