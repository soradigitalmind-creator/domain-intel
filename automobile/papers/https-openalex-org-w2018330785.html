Exposure-response models based on extended follow-up of a cohort mortality study in the automobile industry

Year: 2001

Main topic: asphalt path: automobile

Related topics:

Summary: The results provide further evidence that exposure to metalworking fluids causes cancer among workers in automobile manufacturing. Although airborne exposures declined over the study period, this study suggests that modest risk of several digestive cancers, as well as prostatic cancer and leukemia, may persist at current levels of exposure to water-based metalworking fluids.
