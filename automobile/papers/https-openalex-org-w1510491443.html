‘Peak Car Use’: Understanding the Demise of Automobile Dependence

Year: 2011

Primary subgenre: car ownership

Secondary subgenres: demiseeconomic geographyeconomic growthpolitical science

Summary: This article identifies and discusses the concept of “peak car use”. Car use is declining in the USA, UK, Australia and a range of other relatively wealthy counties. Data are presented and discussed, the decline in car use confirmed and six potential causes identified. The causes include growth in public transport use, hitting the Marchetti wall, reversal of urban sprawl, ageing of cities, growth
