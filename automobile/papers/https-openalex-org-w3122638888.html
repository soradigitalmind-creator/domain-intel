Asymmetric Information and Learning: Evidence from the Automobile Insurance Market

Year: 2005

Primary subgenre: automotive engineering

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: This paper tests the predictions of adverse-selection models using data from the automobile insurance market. I find that, in contrast to what recent research suggests, the evidence is consistent with the presence of informational asymmetries in this market: new customers choosing higher insurance coverage are associated with more accidents.
