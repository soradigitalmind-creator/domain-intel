The Welfare Effects of Misperceived Product Costs: Data and Calibrations from the Automobile Market

Year: 2013

Main topic: automobiles in united

Related topics:

Summary: This analysis exploits new data from the Vehicle Ownership and Alternatives Survey, which elicits beliefs over the financial benefits of owning higher fuel economy vehicles. The data are used to test for underestimation and to document evidence of “MPG Illusion”: consumers think as if fuel costs scale linearly in miles per gallon instead of gallons per mile.
