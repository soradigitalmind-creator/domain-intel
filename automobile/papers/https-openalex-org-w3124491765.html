Social Influence and Consumption: Evidence from the Automobile Purchases of Neighbors

Year: 2008

Main topic: automobile industry

Related topics:

Summary: This study analyzes the automobile purchase behavior of all residents of two Finnish provinces over several years. Using a comprehensive data set with location coordinates at the individual consumer level, it finds that the purchases of neighbors, particularly in the recent past and by those who are geographically most proximate, influence a consumer's purchases of automobiles.
