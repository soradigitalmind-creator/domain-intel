Effect of Electronic Stability Control on Automobile Crash Risk

Year: 2004

Primary subgenre: electronic stability control

Secondary subgenres: environmental healthforensic engineeringinjury preventionoccupational safety and health

Summary: Per vehicle crash involvement rates were compared for otherwise identical vehicle models with and without electronic stability control (ESC) systems. ESC was found to affect single-vehicle crashes to a greater extent than multiple-vehicle crashes, and crashes with fatal injuries to a greater extent than less severe crashes.
