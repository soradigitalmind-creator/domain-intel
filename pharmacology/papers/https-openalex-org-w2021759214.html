P-glycoprotein in the blood-brain barrier of mice influences the brain penetration and pharmacological activity of many drugs.

Year: 1996  DOI ↗

Main topic: clinical pharmacology

Abstract
The mouse mdr1a (also called mdr3) P-GP is abundant in the blood-brain barrier, and its absence in mdr1a (-/-) mice leads to highly increased levels of the drugs ivermectin, vinblastine, digoxin, and cyclosporin A in the brain. We show here that the drugs loperamide, domperidone, and ondansetron are transported substrates for the mouse mdr1a P-GP and its human homologue MDR1.
