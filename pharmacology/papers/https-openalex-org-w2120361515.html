The Melanocortin-4 Receptor: Physiology, Pharmacology, and Pathophysiology

Year: 2010  DOI ↗

Main topic: cell biology

Abstract
The melanocortin-4 receptor (MC4R) was cloned in 1993 by degenerate PCR; however, its function was unknown. Subsequent studies suggest that the MC4R might be involved in regulating energy homeostasis. This hypothesis was confirmed in 1997 by a series of seminal studies in mice. In 1998, human genetic studies demonstrated that mutations in the MC4R gene can cause monogenic obesity.
