Collagen Carbohydrates are not Involved in Collagen-Initiated Platelet Aggregation

Year: 1977  DOI ↗

Main topic: —

Abstract
There is strong evidence that the quaternary (polymeric) structure of collagen is necessary for platelet aggregation. Although the specific groups required are unknown, the carbohydrate moieties have been implicated. To further elucidate their role we have used NaI04 (0.01M, 4°C in the dark for 24 hours) to selectively destroy 75–87% of total carbohydrate »The collagens were characterized by circular dichroism spectra and by polymerization kinetics observed by light scattering.
