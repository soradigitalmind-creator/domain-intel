Abstract
Automotive radars are on the market since 1999, both in the frequency range around 24 GHz as well as 76.5 GHz, with a new frequency band ranging from 77 to 81 GHz intended for medium and short-range sensors. The choice and design of the respective sensor antennas are determined by the requirement for high gain and low loss combined with small size and depth for vehicle integration, the challenges by the millimeter-wave frequency range, and a great cost pressure for this commercial application.
