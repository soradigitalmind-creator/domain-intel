Robotics, Motor Learning, and Neurologic Recovery

Year: 2004  DOI ↗

Main topic: human-computer interaction

Abstract
Robotic devices are helping shed light on human motor control in health and injury. By using robots to apply novel force fields to the arm, investigators are gaining insight into how the nervous system models its external dynamic environment. The nervous system builds internal models gradually by experience and uses them in combination with impedance and feedback control strategies.
