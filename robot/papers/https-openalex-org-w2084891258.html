The Calculation of Robot Dynamics Using Articulated-Body Inertias

Year: 1983  DOI ↗

Main topic: robotic arm

Abstract
This paper describes a new method for calculating the acceleration of a robot in response to given actuator forces. The method is applicable to open-loop kinematic chains containing revolute and prismatic joints. The algorithm is based on recursive formulas involving quantities called articulated-body inertias, which represent the inertia properties of collections of rigid bodies connected together by joints allowing constrained relative motion between the bodies.
