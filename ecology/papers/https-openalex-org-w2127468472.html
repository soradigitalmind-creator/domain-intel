Chemical Ecology and Social Parasitism in Ants

Year: 2001  DOI ↗

Main topic: ecology evolution

Abstract
The chemical strategies by which parasites manage to break into the social fortresses of ants offer a fascinating theme in chemical ecology. Semiochemicals used for interindividual nestmate recognition are also involved in the mechanisms of tolerance and association between the species, and social parasites exploit these mechanisms.
