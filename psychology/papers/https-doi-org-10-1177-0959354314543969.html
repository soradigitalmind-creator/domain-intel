Psychology oblivious to psychology: Some limits on our capacity for processing psychology in society

Year: 2014

Main topic: history of psychology

Related topics:

Summary: George A. Miller’s presidential address to the American Psychological Association in 1969 urged psychologists “to give psychology away” in order to solve social problems related to human health and welfare, and has since become a standard reference for calls for social responsibility. But Miller also envisioned a psychological revolution that would ultimately change humankind’s concept of itself.
