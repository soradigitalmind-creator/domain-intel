Crypts Are the Site of Intestinal Fluid and Electrolyte Secretion

Year: 1982  DOI ↗

Main topic: electrolyte membrane

Abstract
The site of adenosine 3',5'-monophosphate-mediated fluid and electrolyte secretion across mammalian large intestine was found to be the crypts of Lieberkühn by means of two techniques. First, the formation of fluid droplets was visualized on the oil-covered mucosal surface directly over crypt duct openings when secretion was stimulated.
