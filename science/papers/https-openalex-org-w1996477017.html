Year: 1995

Primary subgenre: aggression

Secondary subgenres: basic sciencecognitive psychologyevolutionary ecology

Summary: Abstract Psychological science is currently in conceptual disarray, characterized by unconnected mini-theories and isolated empirical findings. We lack a theory of the functional properties of the human mind that could provide the needed integration—a theory about what the mechanisms of mind are "designed" to do. Evolutionary psychology provides the conceptual tools for emerging from this fragment
