Women in science: career processes and outcomes

Year: 2004

Primary subgenre: diversity and career in medicine

Secondary subgenres: sociologyscience and engineering

Summary: Why do so few women choose a career in science - even as they move into medicine and law in ever-greater numbers? This comprehensive study of gender differences in science careers provides a systematic account of how US youths are selected into and out of science education in early life and how social forces affect career outcomes later in the science labour market.
