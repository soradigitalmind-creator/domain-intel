National differences in gender–science stereotypes predict national sex differences in science and math achievement

Year: 2009

Primary subgenre: demographic economics

Secondary subgenres: social psychology

Summary: About 70% of more than half a million Implicit Association Tests completed by citizens of 34 countries revealed expected implicit stereotypes associating science with males more than with females. We discovered that nation-level implicit stereotypes predicted nation-level sex differences in 8th-grade science and mathematics achievement.
