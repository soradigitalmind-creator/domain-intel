Politicization of Science in the Public Sphere

Year: 2012

Primary subgenre: climate change communication and perception

Secondary subgenres: lawpolitics

Summary: This study explores time trends in public trust in science in the United States from 1974 to 2010. More precisely, I test Mooney’s (2005) claim that conservatives in the United States have become increasingly distrustful of science. Using data from the 1974 to 2010 General Social Survey, I examine group differences in trust in science and group-specific change in these attitudes over time. Results
