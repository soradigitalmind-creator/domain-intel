Attitudes towards science: A review of the literature and its implications

Year: 2003

Primary subgenre: science education

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: This article offers a review of the major literature about attitudes to science and its implications over the past 20 years. It argues that the continuing decline in numbers choosing to study science at the point of choice requires a research focus on students' attitudes to science if the nature of the problem is to be understood and remediated.
