Abstract
Epidermal growth factor (EGF)-enhanced protein kinase activity in plasma membrane preparations of A-431 human epidermoid carcinoma cells was shown to involve the phosphorylation of tyrosine residues. Phosphotyrosine was detected in both endogenous membrane proteins and in histone when added as an exogenous protein substrate.
