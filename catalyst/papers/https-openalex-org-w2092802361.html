Efficient Dehydrogenation of Formic Acid Using an Iron Catalyst

Year: 2011

Primary subgenre: nanomaterial-based catalyst

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Hydrogen is one of the essential reactants in the chemical industry, though its generation from renewable sources and storage in a safe and reversible manner remain challenging. Formic acid (HCO(2)H or FA) is a promising source and storage material in this respect. Here, we present a highly active iron catalyst system for the liberation of H(2) from FA.
