Direct Catalytic Conversion of Cellulose into Ethylene Glycol Using Nickel‐Promoted Tungsten Carbide Catalysts

Year: 2008

Primary subgenre: nanomaterial-based catalyst

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Nickel saves dimes: The expense of using precious-metal catalysts is avoided in the high-yielding conversion of cellulose to ethylene glycol (see picture; AC=activated carbon). This process occurs in up to 29 % yield over a tungsten carbide catalyst, and in up to 61 % yield when the catalyst is promoted with a small amount of nickel.
