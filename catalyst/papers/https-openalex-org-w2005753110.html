Hydroformylation of alkenes by use of rhodium complex catalysts

Year: 1968

Primary subgenre: organometallic complex synthesis and catalysis

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: The use of complexes of rhodium of the type trans-RhX(CO)(PR3)2, (X = halogen, R = aryl) as hydroformylation catalysts for alkenes is studied. It is shown that an inhibition period is removed by addition of hydrogen halide acceptors and that the halide complex undergoes hydrogenolysis to form a hydrido-species.
