Applications of Transition‐Metal Catalysts to Textile and Wood‐Pulp Bleaching

Year: 2005

Primary subgenre: hydrogen peroxide

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: From an economic perspective, textile and paper bleaching are amongst the most important oxidation processes. The removal of unwanted chromophores, be it stains on cloths or residual lignin in wood pulp, consumes more than 60 % of the world production of hydrogen peroxide. However, existing technologies have their limitations. At ambient temperature, hydrogen peroxide gives little stain bleaching
