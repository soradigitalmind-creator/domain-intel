Influence of Oxygen Evolution during Water Oxidation on the Surface of Perovskite Oxide Catalysts

Year: 2012

Primary subgenre: nanomaterial-based catalyst

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Perovskites such as Ba0.5Sr0.5Co0.8Fe0.2O3−δ (BSCF82) can be highly active for the oxygen evolution reaction (OER) upon water oxidation in alkaline solution. Here we report that BSCF82 can quickly undergo amorphization of its surface at OER potentials, which is accompanied by reduced surface concentrations of Ba2+ and Sr2+ ions as well as increased pseudocapacitive and OER currents.
