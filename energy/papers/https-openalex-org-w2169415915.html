Constructing Free-Energy Approximations and Generalized Belief Propagation Algorithms

Year: 2005

Primary subgenre:

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Important inference problems in statistical physics, computer vision, error-correcting coding theory, and artificial intelligence can all be reformulated as the computation of marginal probabilities on factor graphs. The belief propagation (BP) algorithm is an efficient way to solve these problems that is exact when the factor graph is a tree, but only approximate when the factor graph has cycles.
