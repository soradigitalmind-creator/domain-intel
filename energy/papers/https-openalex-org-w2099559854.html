Spectral Energy Distributions of T Tauri Stars with Passive Circumstellar Disks

Year: 1997

Primary subgenre:

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: We derive hydrostatic, radiative equilibrium models for passive disks surrounding T Tauri stars. Each disk is encased by an optically thin layer of superheated dust grains. This layer reemits directly to space about half the stellar energy it absorbs. The other half is emitted inward and regulates the interior temperature of the disk. The heated disk flares.
