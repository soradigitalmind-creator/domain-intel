Gaussian-2 theory for molecular energies of first- and second-row compounds

Year: 1991

Main topic: renewable energy

Related topics:

Summary: The Gaussian-2 theoretical procedure (G2 theory), based on ab initio molecular orbital theory, for calculation of molecular energies (atomization energies, ionization potentials, electron affinities, and proton affinities) of compounds containing first- (Li–F) and second-row atoms (Na–Cl) is presented. This new theoretical procedure adds three features to G1 theory [J. Chem. Phys. 90, 5622 (1989)]
