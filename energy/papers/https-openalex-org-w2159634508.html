Electrode-Reducing Microorganisms That Harvest Energy from Marine Sediments

Year: 2002

Main topic: atomic energy agency

Related topics:

Summary: Energy in the form of electricity can be harvested from marine sediments by placing a graphite electrode (the anode) in the anoxic zone and connecting it to a graphite cathode in the overlying aerobic water.
