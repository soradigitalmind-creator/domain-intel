Direct-Write Piezoelectric Polymeric Nanogenerator with High Energy Conversion Efficiency

Year: 2010

Primary subgenre:

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Nanogenerators capable of converting energy from mechanical sources to electricity with high effective efficiency using low-cost, nonsemiconducting, organic nanomaterials are attractive for many applications, including energy harvesters. In this work, near-field electrospinning is used to direct-write poly(vinylidene fluoride) (PVDF) nanofibers with in situ mechanical stretch and electrical poling
