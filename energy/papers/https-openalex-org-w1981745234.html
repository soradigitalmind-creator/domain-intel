A complete basis set model chemistry. I. The total energies of closed-shell atoms and hydrides of the first-row elements

Year: 1988

Primary subgenre:

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: The major source of errror in most ab initio calculations of molecular energies is the truncation of the one-electron basis set. A complete basis set model chemistry is defined to include corrections for basis set truncation errors. This model uses double zeta plus polarization level atomic pair natural orbital basis sets to calculate molecular self-consistent-field (SCF) energies and correlation
