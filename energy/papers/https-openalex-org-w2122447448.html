On Energy, Personality, and Health: Subjective Vitality as a Dynamic Reflection of Well‐Being

Year: 1997

Main topic: atomic energy agency

Related topics:

Summary: In this article, we examine subjective vitality, a positive feeling of aliveness and energy, in six studies. Subjective vitality is hypothesized to reflect organismic well-being and thus should covary with both psychological and somatic factors that impact the energy available to the self.
