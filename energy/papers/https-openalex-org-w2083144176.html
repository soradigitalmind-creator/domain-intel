The Dynamic Energy Landscape of Dihydrofolate Reductase Catalysis

Year: 2006

Primary subgenre:

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: We used nuclear magnetic resonance relaxation dispersion to characterize higher energy conformational substates of Escherichia coli dihydrofolate reductase. Each intermediate in the catalytic cycle samples low-lying excited states whose conformations resemble the ground-state structures of preceding and following intermediates. Substrate and cofactor exchange occurs through these excited substates
