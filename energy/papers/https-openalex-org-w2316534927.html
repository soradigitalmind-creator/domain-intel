Changes in Energy Expenditure Resulting from Altered Body Weight

Year: 1995

Primary subgenre:

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Maintenance of a reduced or elevated body weight is associated with compensatory changes in energy expenditure, which oppose the maintenance of a body weight that is different from the usual weight. These compensatory changes may account for the poor long-term efficacy of treatments for obesity.
