Reduced Rate of Energy Expenditure as a Risk Factor for Body-Weight Gain

Year: 1988

Main topic: atomic energy agency

Related topics:

Summary: The contribution of reduced energy expenditure to the development of obesity has been a point of controversy. We measured 24-hour energy expenditure (adjusted for body composition, age, and sex), in a respiratory chamber, in 95 southwestern American Indians.
