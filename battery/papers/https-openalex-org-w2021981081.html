An Amorphous Red Phosphorus/Carbon Composite as a Promising Anode Material for Sodium Ion Batteries

Year: 2013

Primary subgenre: composite material

Secondary subgenres: sodium-ion battery

Summary: An amorphous red phosphorus/carbon composite is obtained through a facile and simple ball milling process, and its electrochemical performance as an anode material for Na ion batteries is evaluated. The composite shows excellent electrochemical performance including a high specific capacity of 1890 mA h g(-1), negligible capacity fading over 30 cycles, an ideal redox potential (0.4 V vs. Na/Na(+))
