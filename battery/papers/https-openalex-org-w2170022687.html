A Critical Size of Silicon Nano‐Anodes for Lithium Rechargeable Batteries

Year: 2010

Primary subgenre: lithium battery

Secondary subgenres: advancements in battery materialcomposite materialorganic chemistryphysical chemistry

Summary: Well-dispersed Si nanocrystals with sizes of approximately 5, 10, and 20 nm were prepared in reverse micelles at high pressure and 380 °C and investigated as anode materials for lithium batteries. The 10 nm sized nanocrystals show a first charge capacity y of 3380 mAh g−1 and the highest capacity retention of 81 % after 40 cycles, which can be increased to 96 % by carbon coating (see picture).
