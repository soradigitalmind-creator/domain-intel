A High‐Energy Room‐Temperature Sodium‐Sulfur Battery

Year: 2013

Primary subgenre: inorganic chemistry

Secondary subgenres: physical chemistrypower

Summary: Employing small sulfur molecules as the active cathode component for room-temperature Na-S batteries, reveals a novel mechanism that is verified for the batteries' electrochemistry. The sulfur cathode enables a complete two-electron reaction to form Na2 S, bringing a tripled specific capacity and an increased specific energy compared with traditional high-temperature Na-S batteries.
