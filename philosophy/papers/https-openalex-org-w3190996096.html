The Twenty-Five Years of Philosophy

Year: 2012

Primary subgenre: kantian philosophy and modern interpretations

Secondary subgenres: history

Summary: Kant declared that philosophy began in 1781 with his Critique of Pure Reason. In 1806 Hegel announced that philosophy had now been completed. Eckart Förster examines the reasons behind these claims and assesses the steps that led in such a short time from Kant's "beginning" to Hegel's "end."
