The Philosophy of Biology

Year: 2004

Primary subgenre: natural philosophy

Secondary subgenres: philosophy and history of science

Summary: Is life different from the non-living? If so, how? And how, in that case, does biology as the study of living things differ from other sciences? These questions are traced through an exploration of episodes in the history of biology and philosophy.
