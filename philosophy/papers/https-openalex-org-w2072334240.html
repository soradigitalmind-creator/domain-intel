The Philosophy of Statistics

Year: 2000

Primary subgenre: philosophy and history of science

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: This paper puts forward an overall view of statistics. It is argued that statistics is the study of uncertainty. The many demonstrations that uncertainties can only combine according to the rules of the probability calculus are summarized. The conclusion is that statistical inference is firmly based on probability alone. Progress is therefore dependent on the construction of a probability model; m
