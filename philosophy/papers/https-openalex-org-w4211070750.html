The Philosophy of Social Practices

Year: 2002

Primary subgenre: philosophy of social science

Secondary subgenres: 20th-century philosophy

Summary: This is a systematic philosophical and conceptual study of the notion of a social practice. Raimo Tuomela explains social practices in terms of the interlocking mental states of the agents; he shows how social practices (for example customs and traditions) are 'building blocks of society'; and he offers a clear and powerful account of the way in which social institutions are constructed from these
