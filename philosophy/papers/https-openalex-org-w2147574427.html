The things we do with words: Ilongot speech acts and speech act theory in philosophy

Year: 1982

Primary subgenre: action

Secondary subgenres: linguisticsanthropology

Summary: ABSTRACT I begin by introducing the Ilongots and some of their attitudes toward speech. Whereas most modem theorists think of language as a tool designed primarily to “express” or to “refer,” Ilongots think of language first in terms of action. They see commands as the exemplary act of speech, displaying less concern for the subjective meanings that an utterance conveys than for the social context
