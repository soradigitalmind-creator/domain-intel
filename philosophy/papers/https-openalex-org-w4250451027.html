Consciousness and Revolution in Soviet Philosophy

Year: 1991

Primary subgenre: critical theory and philosophy

Secondary subgenres: relationconsciousnesslawpolitical science

Summary: This 1991 book is a critical study of the philosophical culture of the USSR, and the first substantial treatment of a Soviet philosopher's work by a Western author. The book identifies a tradition within Soviet Marxism that has produced significant theories of the nature of the self and human activity, of the origins of value and meaning, and of the relation of thought and language.
