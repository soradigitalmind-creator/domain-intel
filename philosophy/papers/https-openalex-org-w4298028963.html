Why Does Language Matter to Philosophy?

Year: 1975

Primary subgenre: philosophy of computer science

Secondary subgenres: philosophy of languagemetaphysicswestern philosophy

Summary: Many people find themselves dissatisfied with recent linguistic philosophy, and yet know that language has always mattered deeply to philosophy and must in some sense continue to do so. Ian Hacking considers here some dozen case studies in the history of philosophy to show the different ways in which language has been important, and the consequences for the development of the subject.
