Mechanics of Swimming and Flying

Year: 1981

Primary subgenre: fluid mechanics

Secondary subgenres: propulsionexperimental and theoretical physics studiestheoretical physics

Summary: This book provides a clear and concise summary of the fluid dynamics of the locomotion of living organisms. The biological phenomena described in detail range from the swimming of bacteria and fish to the flying of insects and birds. The breadth of treatment requires the study of two basic fluid-dynamical regimes. In the first case, that of small organisms, the viscosity of the fluid is paramount
