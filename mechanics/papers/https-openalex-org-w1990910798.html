Quantum Mechanics of Fractional-Spin Particles

Year: 1982

Primary subgenre: statistical mechanics

Secondary subgenres: angular momentummechanicsquantum

Summary: Composites formed from charged particles and vortices in (2+1)-dimensional models, or flux tubes in three-dimensional models, can have any (fractional) angular momentum. The statistics of these objects, like their spin, interpolates continuously between the usual boson and fermion cases. How this works for two-particle quantum mechanics is discussed here.
