The hierarchical structure and mechanics of plant materials

Year: 2012

Primary subgenre: composite material

Secondary subgenres: material sciencemechanics

Summary: The cell walls in plants are made up of just four basic building blocks: cellulose (the main structural fibre of the plant kingdom) hemicellulose, lignin and pectin. Although the microstructure of plant cell walls varies in different types of plants, broadly speaking, cellulose fibres reinforce a matrix of hemicellulose and either pectin or lignin.
