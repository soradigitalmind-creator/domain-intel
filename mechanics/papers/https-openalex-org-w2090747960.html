Statistical Mechanics of Violent Relaxation in Stellar Systems

Year: 1967

Primary subgenre: statistical mechanics

Secondary subgenres: classical mechanics

Summary: The violently changing gravitational field of a newly formed galaxy is effective in changing the statistics of stellar orbits. The equilibrium distribution under this encounterless relaxation is found by use of a fourth type of statistics related to both Fermi–Dirac statistics and equipartition of energy per unit mass. In the relevant limit this becomes Maxwell's distribution but with temperature
