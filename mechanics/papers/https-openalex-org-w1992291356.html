Precision Tests of Quantum Mechanics

Year: 1989

Primary subgenre: statistical mechanics

Secondary subgenres: mathematical analysisnonlinear systemquantum dissipationstatistical physics

Summary: It is proposed to set stringent limits on possible nonlinear corrections to ordinary quantum mechanics by searching for the detuning of resonant transitions. A suggested nonlinear generalization of quantum mechanics is used to show that such detuning would be expected in the rf transition in $^{9}\mathrm{Be}^{+}$ ions that is used to set frequency standards.
