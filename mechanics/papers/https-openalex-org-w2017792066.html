Structural Biological Materials: Critical Mechanics-Materials Connections

Year: 2013

Primary subgenre: mechanics

Secondary subgenres: biological materialcell mechanics

Summary: The Building Blocks of Life Biological organisms are often limited in the resources that they can use to make structural materials. Primary building blocks may be weak or brittle materials, such as minerals and biopolymers, and processing conditions by default have to be mild. Despite this, a wide range of strong and tough structures exist, including shells, bones, quills, and fibers. Meyers et al
