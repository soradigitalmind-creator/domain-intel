Fluid Mechanics of Microrheology

Year: 2010

Primary subgenre: fluid mechanics

Secondary subgenres: classical mechanics

Summary: In microrheology, the local and bulk mechanical properties of a complex fluid are extracted from the motion of probe particles embedded within it. In passive microrheology, particles are forced by thermal fluctuations and probe linear viscoelasticity, whereas active microrheology involves forcing probes externally and can be extended out of equilibrium to the nonlinear regime.
