Women Physicians and Promotion in Academic Medicine

Year: 2020

Primary subgenre: medicine

Secondary subgenres: academic medicinegeography

Summary: Over a 35-year period, women physicians in academic medical centers were less likely than men to be promoted to the rank of associate or full professor or to be appointed to department chair, and there was no apparent narrowing in the gap over time. (Funded by the University of Kansas Medical Center Joy McCann Professorship for Women in Medicine and the American Association of University Women.).
