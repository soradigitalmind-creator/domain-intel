Aquaporins in the Kidney: From Molecules to Medicine

Year: 2002

Primary subgenre: biochemistry

Secondary subgenres: biologychemistry

Summary: The discovery of aquaporin-1 (AQP1) answered the long-standing biophysical question of how water specifically crosses biological membranes. In the kidney, at least seven aquaporins are expressed at distinct sites. AQP1 is extremely abundant in the proximal tubule and descending thin limb and is essential for urinary concentration.
