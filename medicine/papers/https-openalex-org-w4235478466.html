Journal of Sports Science and Medicine

Year: 2014

Primary subgenre: medicine

Secondary subgenres: anesthesia

Summary: Salbutamol, a short-acting beta-agonist, is a bronchodilator that works by reversing airway smooth muscle bronchoconstriction. The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) guidelines allow athletes a maximum of 1600 μg inhaled salbutamol; however, there have been no conclusive studies evaluating how this dose affects exercise performance. The current study investigated the use of 1600 μg inhaled salbutamol
