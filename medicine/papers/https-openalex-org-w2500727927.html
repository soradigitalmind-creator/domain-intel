Artemisinin—A Gift from Traditional Chinese Medicine to the World (Nobel Lecture)

Year: 2016

Primary subgenre: medicine

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Malaria has long been a devastating and life-threatening global epidemic disease in human history. Artemisinin, the active substance against malaria, was first isolated and tested in the 1970s in China. The important role played by traditional Chinese medicine in the discovery of artemisinin is described by Y. Tu in her Nobel Lecture.
