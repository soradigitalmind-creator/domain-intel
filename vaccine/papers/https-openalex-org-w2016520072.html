The Effects of Anti-Vaccine Conspiracy Theories on Vaccination Intentions

Year: 2014  DOI ↗

Main topic: vaccine efficacy

Abstract
The current studies investigated the potential impact of anti-vaccine conspiracy beliefs, and exposure to anti-vaccine conspiracy theories, on vaccination intentions. In Study 1, British parents completed a questionnaire measuring beliefs in anti-vaccine conspiracy theories and the likelihood that they would have a fictitious child vaccinated.
