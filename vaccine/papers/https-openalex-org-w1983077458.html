Understanding vaccine safety information from the Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System

Year: 2004  DOI ↗

Main topic: vaccine safety

Abstract
The Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS) is administered by the Food and Drug Administration and CDC and is a key component of postlicensure vaccine safety surveillance. Its primary function is to detect early warning signals and generate hypotheses about possible new vaccine adverse events or changes in frequency of known ones.
