The "Stern Review" on the Economics of Climate Change

Year: 2006

Main topic: public economics

Related topics:

natural resource economics
Abstract
How much and how fast should we react to the threat of global warming? The Stern Review argues that the damages from climate change are large, and that nations should undertake sharp and immediate reductions in greenhouse-gas emissions. An examination of the Review’s radical revision of the economics of climate change finds, however, that it depends decisively on the assumption of a near-zero time discount rate combined with a specific utility function.
