Organic semiconductors: A theoretical characterization of the basic parameters governing charge transport

Year: 2002

Primary subgenre: charge carrier

Secondary subgenres: chemical physicsconjugated systemelectron transport chainmolecule

Summary: Organic semiconductors based on pi-conjugated oligomers and polymers constitute the active elements in new generations of plastic (opto)electronic devices. The performance of these devices depends largely on the efficiency of the charge-transport processes; at the microscopic level, one of the major parameters governing the transport properties is the amplitude of the electronic transfer integrals
