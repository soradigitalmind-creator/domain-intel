Temperature Dependence of the Energy Gap in Semiconductors

Year: 1951

Primary subgenre: absorption edge

Secondary subgenres: polarsemiconductor quantum structures and devicesband gapintrinsic semiconductor

Summary: The problem treated is the effect of lattice vibrations in producing a shift of the energy levels which results in a temperature dependent variation of the energy gap in semiconductors. Calculations for silicon and germanium give results of the same order of magnitude as the observed temperature dependent shift of the absorption band edge.
