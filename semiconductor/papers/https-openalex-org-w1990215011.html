Electromigration failure modes in aluminum metallization for semiconductor devices

Year: 1969

Primary subgenre: compound semiconductor

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Two wear-out type failure modes involving aluminum metallization for semiconductor devices are described. Both modes involve mass transport by momentum exchange between conducting electrons and metal ions. The first failure mode is the formation of an electrically open circuit due to the condensation of vacancies in the aluminum to form voids. The second is the formation of etch pits into silicon
