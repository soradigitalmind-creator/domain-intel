Production of large-area single-crystal wafers of cubic SiC for semiconductor devices

Year: 1983

Primary subgenre: wide-bandgap semiconductor

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: A reproducible process is described for growing a thick single-crystal layer of cubic SiC on a single-crystal Si wafer by chemical vapor deposition. A buffer layer, grown in situ, is used between the cubic SiC and the Si substrate to minimize the effect of lattice mismatch. Layers of up to 34 μm thick and several cm2 in area have been grown.
