The Role of Driving Energy and Delocalized States for Charge Separation in Organic Semiconductors

Year: 2012

Primary subgenre: organic semiconductor

Secondary subgenres: charge carrierchemical physicsmolecule

Summary: The electron-hole pair created via photon absorption in organic photoconversion systems must overcome the Coulomb attraction to achieve long-range charge separation. We show that this process is facilitated through the formation of excited, delocalized band states.
