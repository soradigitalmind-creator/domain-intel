Localized Surface Plasmon Resonance in Semiconductor Nanocrystals

Year: 2018

Primary subgenre: localized surface plasmon

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: Localized surface plasmon resonance (LSPR) in semiconductor nanocrystals (NCs) that results in resonant absorption, scattering, and near field enhancement around the NC can be tuned across a wide optical spectral range from visible to far-infrared by synthetically varying doping level, and post synthetically via chemical oxidation and reduction, photochemical control, and electrochemical control.
