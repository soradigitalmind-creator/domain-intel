Nonlinear optics of normal-mode-coupling semiconductor microcavities

Year: 1999

Primary subgenre: strong light-matter interactions

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: The authors review the nonlinear optical properties of semiconductor quantum wells that are grown inside high-Q Bragg-mirror microcavities. Light-matter coupling in this system is particularly pronounced, leading in the linear regime to a polaritonic mixing of the excitonic quantum well resonance and the single longitudinal cavity mode.
