Spectroscopy of the solid-state analogues of the hydrogen atom: donors and acceptors in semiconductors

Year: 1981

Primary subgenre: semiconductor quantum structures and devices

Secondary subgenres: binding energy

Summary: Under suitable circumstances imperfections in semiconductors can bind electrons (holes) with a binding energy small compared to the intrinsic energy gap of the host; the wavefunctions characterising the energy levels of the imperfection are extended over many lattice spacings. This review discusses the electronic energy levels of chemical impurities in the classic group IV elemental and the III-V
