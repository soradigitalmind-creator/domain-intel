Efficient electrical spin injection from a magnetic metal/tunnel barrier contact into a semiconductor

Year: 2002

Primary subgenre: electrical contacts

Secondary subgenres: spinspin polarization

Summary: We report electrical spin injection from a ferromagnetic metal contact into a semiconductor light emitting diode structure with an injection efficiency of 30% which persists to room temperature. The Schottky barrier formed at the Fe/AlGaAs interface provides a natural tunnel barrier for injection of spin polarized electrons under reverse bias.
