Photoelectrochemical sterilization of microbial cells by semiconductor powders

Year: 1985

Primary subgenre: compound semiconductor

Secondary subgenres:

Summary: We report the novel concept of photochemical sterilization. Microbial cells were killed photoelectrochemically with semiconductor powder (platinum-loaded titanium oxide, TiO2/Pt). Coenzyme A, (CoA) in the whole cells was photo-electrochemically oxidized and, as a result, the respiration of cells was inhibited. Inhibition of respiratory activity caused death of the cells.
